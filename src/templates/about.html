English Summary

Discourse on Articles (Sit'q'ua Art'ront'at'vis) is an Old Georgian grammatical treatise preserved in two manuscript witnesses (A and S). The text addresses a fundamental problem in Georgian-Greek translation: how to render the Greek definite article (ἄρθρον) in Georgian, a language that lacks articles.

The treatise systematically presents the Greek article paradigms across all genders, numbers, and cases, explaining their semantic functions and the theological implications of their presence or absence—particularly in Christological contexts where the article distinguishes between "God" (ὁ θεός, the one God) and "a god" (θεός).

This digital edition, encoded in TEI-XML, provides the critical text with apparatus, a comprehensive lexicon of grammatical terminology, and indices linking Georgian terms to their Greek equivalents.
